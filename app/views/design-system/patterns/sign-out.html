Leaving a service (sign out)

Allow users to leave a service at the right time.

How to use these patterns

Do research to understand at what point in a service a user will need to leave.

For public-facing services it may be better to describe it as leaving a service rather then signing out. Users might not feel they have signed in to a service.

Only give users the option to leave mid-service if they can save progress and carry on with the service at another time.

Consider the environment users will use your service.

In page

When to use this pattern

When a user comes to a certain point in your service and the most important action is to leave. This can still be used with a persistent link elsewhere.

At the end of a service.

When a user is likely to be working from a public space and is inputting sensitive data.

Under header

When to use this pattern

When signing out of a service is best practice but not crucial for the user.

After leaving the service

Take users to a confirmation page that includes:

  • a confirmation that the sign out has been successful
  • gives them the option to sign back in again

User research

Users are confident in using a 'leave the service' button at the end of a transaction. Users mention they want to make sure they leave a service securely.
