Images and illustrations

Please contact the central design team (design@digital.homeoffice.gov.uk) for advice on image use and to commision illustrations.

Use of images

Images can add visual interest to a page. They should be used with consideration and be relevant to the content. Images must not be used to fill space or as decoration.

Where images are used, the photographic style should focus on people, be inclusive and reflect the diversity of the people we serve. Do not use clip art or cliched stock photography. Make images accessible. Do not place text on top of an image, use text as an image, or add unnecessary filters or effects.

All images must be free from usage restrictions and permissible for use.

Use of illustrations and illustration style

An illustration can consist of an icon, scene or character, and can help users understand complex, detailed or ambiguous questions.

Similar to image use, illustrations must be relevant to the content and help users understand what they need to do.

Illustrations must have a consistent style to create reassurance across online and print.
